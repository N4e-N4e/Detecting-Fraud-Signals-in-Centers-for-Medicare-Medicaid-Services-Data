### Description:

General Payments Data

- All general (non-research, non-ownership related) payments from 2021-2023 program years [January 1, 2021 – December 31, 2023]

In 2013, the US initiated a national disclosure program known as Open Payments under the 2010 Physician Payments Sunshine Act for a more transparent and accountable health care system.8 The manufacturers of drug, device, biological, or medical supplies in federal health care programs are mandated to report to CMS any payments or transfers of value of 10 dollars or more to physicians and/or teaching hospitals [1](https://journals.lww.com/annalsofsurgery/fulltext/2023/09000/open_payments_data_analysis_of_general_and.11.aspx)

CMS classifies general (nonresearch) payments into 14 categories (natures of payments): food and beverage, travel and lodging, education, consulting fee, compensation for services other than consulting, honoraria, compensation for serving as faculty or as a speaker for a nonaccredited/noncertified continuing education program, gift, royalty or license, grant, entertainment, current or prospective ownership or investment interest, compensation for serving as faculty or as a speaker for an accredited or certified continuing education program, and charitable contribution. Total amount of payments was summed per each individual surgeon. US states and territories were also examined for numbers and the total amount of general payments. Medical manufacturers and companies were categorized into biological, device, drug, medical supply, and multiple. Research and teaching hospital payments were excluded from this study. [1](https://journals.lww.com/annalsofsurgery/fulltext/2023/09000/open_payments_data_analysis_of_general_and.11.aspx)
